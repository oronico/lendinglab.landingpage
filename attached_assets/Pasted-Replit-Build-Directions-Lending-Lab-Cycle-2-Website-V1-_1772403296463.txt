Replit Build Directions. Lending Lab Cycle 2 Website (V1)
0) Goal and non-negotiables

Build a production-ready website for interested schools that:

Clearly explains Cycle 2 products and requirements.

Stops unqualified borrowers early with hard eligibility gates.

Captures qualified leads cleanly and securely.

Hands off to the correct next step (JotForm application or scheduling link).

Is mobile-first, fast, accessible, and feels polished.

Non-negotiables

No prototype vibes. No placeholder copy. No dead links.

No leaking PII in logs. No public lead endpoints.

Eligibility rules must match Cycle 2. If a rule changes, it should be editable in one place.

5) Eligibility logic (this is the core protection layer)

Implement eligibility as a rules engine with three outcomes:

Not Eligible (hard stops)

Flagged for Review (possible but needs human diligence)

Qualified to Apply

A) Hard stop rules (Not Eligible)

Make these immediate disqualifiers:

No business bank account for school operations

Commingling personal and business funds

No QuickBooks Online (for LOC and operating schools). For Year 0, allow “adopt before closing” only if you explicitly choose that policy

No tuition contracts for all currently enrolled students

Payroll via Venmo, cash, or peer-to-peer apps

No legal business registration or cannot prove good standing

Home-based school if your policy is “ineligible” this cycle

Current litigation or unresolved tax liens, if you choose to include these as exclusions

B) Flagged rules (Flagged for Review)

Examples:

FICO is between minimum and preferred

Less than 12 months operating history, but otherwise strong

Revenue below $100k for LOC but they request LOC anyway

Board independence unclear or borderline

Past due bills occurred but now current

Reconciliations exist but not current to last closed month

C) Qualified rules

Must satisfy:

Product-specific minimums

Clear attestations for QBO, reconciled books, tuition contracts, payroll provider, no commingling

Enrollment verification approach acknowledged

D) Make thresholds editable in one config file

Create a rules.ts or config.ts where these values live:

Min months operating

Min revenue for LOC

FICO thresholds

DSR cap percent for Year 0 and Operating

Home-based allowed true/false

QBO hard stop true/false by product

6) The “X% of revenue” rule (implement as ability-to-repay)

Do not use “loan as % of projected revenue” alone. Use an annual debt service cap so term length changes do not break the rule.

Required fields in the wizard for this calculation

Projected Year 1 underwritten revenue (exclude philanthropy). Provide guidance text.

Requested loan amount and term (4–6 years)

Product type selection

Calculation approach (implement in code)

Compute annual payment for requested amount at current rate

Compare annual payment to Underwritten Revenue times DSR cap

Recommended DSR caps (editable):

Year 0: 8% to 10%

Operating: 10% to 14%

If annual debt service exceeds cap:

Outcome is Flagged or Not Eligible depending on how strict you want to be

Show plain-English explanation and suggest a smaller amount

11) Acceptance criteria (definition of done)

The website is “done” when:

A user can understand products and requirements in under 2 minutes.

A user can complete pre-qual in under 3 minutes on mobile.

Ineligible users are blocked by hard gates with clear messaging.

Qualified users are routed to the correct application link.

Leads save to DB. Admin can view them securely.

No PII appears in logs.

Privacy and Terms pages exist and are linked in footer.

The site works on mobile, tablet, desktop. No broken layout.
